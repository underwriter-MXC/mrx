# MRX Competitor SEO + AEO Gap Analysis

**Objective:** identify how MineralRightsXchange (MRX) can leapfrog mineral-rights competitors in Google Search, AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity, Grok, and future LLM search.

**Evidence scope:** public search results and extracted page snippets gathered 2026-06-15. This is not a paid crawl, Lighthouse lab test, Search Console export, or SearchAtlas rank report. Page-speed, index counts, backlinks, and rankings should be measured next with SearchAtlas/GSC/PageSpeed API before committing numeric claims.

## Executive findings

1. **The category is vulnerable to an AEO-first entrant.** Competitors have good transactional pages and trust signals, but few appear to expose comprehensive agent-readable surfaces (`/llms.txt`, markdown exports, canonical answer blocks, citation APIs, or structured entity graph endpoints). Buckhead Energy appears to expose at least some `llms.md` pages; this is the most direct AEO warning sign.
2. **Buckhead Energy is currently the clearest programmatic SEO threat.** Search results show county, state, basin, operator, buyer-comparison, 1031, and other long-tail pages with FAQ blocks, dates, CTAs, and structured templates.
3. **US Mineral Exchange has seller-intent authority and trust.** It has marketplace positioning, blog categories, free guide, client reviews, founder expertise, and strong education around selling and documents.
4. **Enverus owns data-authority messaging.** It claims EnergyLink-backed owner/revenue coverage, direct secure marketplace engagement, verified data, live chat, and mineral analytics at large scale.
5. **TexasFile owns Texas records/data utility.** It ranks around county clerk records, mineral ownership data, probate, plats, statewide searches, pricing, glossary, and FAQs.
6. **MineralWare/MineralTracker own mineral-management SaaS language.** They compete for portfolio-management and trust/family-office authority rather than immediate seller leads.
7. **MRX’s leapfrog opportunity is to combine all three lanes:** buyer/underwriter trust + public educational authority + structured mineral-rights knowledge graph + agent-readable citation infrastructure.

## Competitor battlecards

### EnergyNet

**Observed sources**
- CIPA profile: `https://www.cipa.org/i4a/pages/index.cfm?pageid=373`
- Search result references to EnergyNet as a 24/7 continuous oil and gas property marketplace with due diligence and bidding.

**Visible strengths**
- Established marketplace narrative: real-time internet auction service for oil/gas properties, leases, and minerals.
- Trust/history claims: founded marketplace pattern, representatives in energy hubs, broker/dealer/FINRA framing, substantial historical transaction claims in third-party profile.
- Clear value proposition for sellers/buyers: reserve price, browsing due diligence data, auctions/sealed bids, online access.

**Likely SEO/AEO weaknesses MRX can exploit**
- Marketplace/auction framing is transaction-centric, not owner-education-centric.
- Public educational content surfaced in search is thinner than US Mineral Exchange/Buckhead-style resource libraries.
- Little observed evidence of county/operator/well/entity answer surfaces.

**MRX leapfrog move**
- Build “auction vs direct underwriter review” comparison content with neutral definitions, risks, due-diligence checklist, and owner decision trees.
- Publish AEO-ready answers for “Should I auction my mineral rights?”, “What documents are in a mineral-rights due diligence package?”, and “Auction vs direct buyer vs broker.”

### US Mineral Exchange

**Observed sources**
- Homepage: `https://www.usmineralexchange.com/`
- Blog welcome/article: `https://www.usmineralexchange.com/blog/sell-mineral-rights/welcome-to-us-mineral-exchange`
- Required documents article: `https://www.usmineralexchange.com/blog/sell-mineral-rights/required-documents-to-list-mineral-rights/`

**Visible strengths**
- Strong seller-intent positioning: “Sell Mineral Rights,” marketplace, broad buyer exposure, highest prices.
- Trust assets: founded 2012, Google reviews, client success stories, founder bio with 27+ years, industry mentions.
- Conversion assets: free consultation, free guide PDF, account/listing flow.
- Educational taxonomy: Buy Mineral Rights, Industry News, Mineral Rights Value, Sell Mineral Rights, Sell Royalties.
- Document-preparation content addresses real owner pain.

**Likely SEO/AEO weaknesses MRX can exploit**
- WordPress/blog structure may be less machine-readable than a purpose-built MRX knowledge repository.
- Education is good but can be outflanked with county/entity-specific data, transparent DCF methodology, schema, llms exports, and programmatic guides.
- Marketplace “highest price” framing can be countered by MRX’s “transparent underwriter review, no predatory tactics, no clawbacks, explainable DCF” positioning.

**MRX leapfrog move**
- Publish a superior “documents needed” hub with downloadable checklist, schema FAQ, first-party examples, and an AI assistant that maps user-provided documents to the next step.
- Create comparison pages: marketplace listing vs underwriter review, broker vs direct buyer, multiple-offer process vs transparent DCF.

### Enverus

**Observed sources**
- Product page: `https://www.enverus.com/products/minerals-marketplace`
- Minerals segment page: `https://www.enverus.com/segments/minerals`
- PRNewswire launch article for Minerals Marketplace.

**Visible strengths**
- Massive data-authority language: EnergyLink, owner/revenue coverage, production, ownership, permitting, revenue/JIB statement data.
- Enterprise trust: secure one-to-one marketplace, SOC 2 language, no-fee/no-contract seller message, direct communication.
- Broad lifecycle positioning: buy, sell, manage, forecast, map, audit, alerts.
- Rich claims around wells and analytics create strong entity authority.

**Likely SEO/AEO weaknesses MRX can exploit**
- Enterprise/B2B language may underserve individual mineral owners searching basic questions.
- Paywalled/app-centric value reduces public indexable educational footprint.
- Enverus is authoritative but not necessarily empathetic/localized for inherited-rights, offer-in-hand, out-of-state owners, trustees, and 1031 sellers.

**MRX leapfrog move**
- Publicly explain valuation in owner language: “how underwriters use production, decline curves, lease terms, operator plans, NRI, and commodity prices.”
- Build a public glossary + entity graph that links Enverus-style data concepts to owner decisions without requiring a paid platform login.

### TexasFile

**Observed sources**
- Homepage: `https://www.texasfile.com/`
- Search/data pages: `https://www.texasfile.com/search-all`, `/search/texas/`, `/texas-land-records-data-features/`

**Visible strengths**
- Owns “Texas records” and “county clerk records” data utility.
- Strong data taxonomy: county clerk records, mineral ownership data, plat maps, probate, OCR search, coverage, courthouse directories, glossary, FAQ, pricing.
- Claims coverage across 200+ Texas counties for mineral ownership data and all 254 Texas counties for statewide search.
- Clear utility pages that AI/search engines can understand as data-source pages.

**Likely SEO/AEO weaknesses MRX can exploit**
- Data access/tool platform, not seller conversion/valuation guide.
- Likely less content around “What should I do with inherited rights?” or “Is this offer fair?”
- Does not appear positioned as a full-service underwriter review conversion path.

**MRX leapfrog move**
- Cite public-record workflows and build educational “how to research mineral rights in [county]” pages that link county clerk, RRC/GIS, appraisal district, and MRX review process.
- Build a Texas county knowledge graph: County → Clerk → Appraisal District → RRC district → operators → basins/formations → MRX valuation factors.

### SS&C MineralWare / MineralTracker

**Observed sources**
- MineralWare: `https://www.sscmineralware.com/`
- MineralTracker: `https://www.mineraltracker.com/`

**Visible strengths**
- SaaS/product authority for mineral management: revenue/JIB automation, mapping, reporting, estate/succession planning, audits, institutional/family-office support.
- MineralTracker owner-friendly feature language: audit checks, forecast income, map wells/rigs/permits, store documents, ask an expert.

**Likely SEO/AEO weaknesses MRX can exploit**
- Management software, not sell/valuation conversion-first.
- Less likely to rank for “sell mineral rights now” and offer-in-hand pages.
- Public content depth appears narrower than the feature/product focus.

**MRX leapfrog move**
- Build content for “manage vs sell vs lease vs hold” and “when revenue-audit software is enough vs when to request an underwriter review.”
- Add RAG-backed owner document vault concepts in MRX’s roadmap to compete for management-intent searches without becoming pure SaaS immediately.

**Visible strengths**

**Likely SEO/AEO weaknesses MRX can exploit**

**MRX leapfrog move**
- Treat Buckhead as the benchmark for programmatic SEO volume, but beat it with data depth, stronger schema, structured downloads, llms exports, and first-party MRX underwriting explanation.

## Immediate win list

1. **Ship root AI-crawler surfaces**: `/llms.txt`, `/llms-full.txt`, `/knowledge/index.json`, per-guide `.md` exports, and sitemap references.
2. **Publish competitor-comparison pages**: direct buyer vs broker vs marketplace, auction vs underwriter review, accepting mailed offer vs DCF review.
3. **Build one complete county vertical slice**: Reeves County mineral rights with county records links, RRC links, active operators, basin/formations, FAQ, schema, MRX CTA, markdown export.
4. **Build one operator vertical slice**: EOG Resources / Permian Basin mineral rights example with RRC/operator links, documents needed, valuation drivers, FAQ, schema.
5. **Add answer-block templates** for every page: concise paragraph answer, bullets, “why it matters,” “what to do next,” source list, disclaimer.
6. **Add trust/entity schema**: Organization, ProfessionalService, Service, FAQPage, BreadcrumbList, Article, Dataset where applicable, and `sameAs` social profiles.
7. **Measure competitors properly next**: SearchAtlas keyword gap, PageSpeed/Lighthouse, sitemap/index counts, schema validation, backlink/mention sources, AI visibility checks.

## Strategic moat recommendations

1. **MRX Mineral Rights Knowledge Graph** — entity store connecting owners, counties, parcels, operators, wells, leases, permits, production, acreage, basins, formations, valuations, and transactions.
2. **Citation-ready public data pages** — not raw private owner data; public educational summaries with source attribution to RRC, county clerks, appraisal districts, EIA, and MRX methodology.
3. **Compliance-gated AI content factory** — SearchAtlas/AI can draft, but publish only after factual, legal/tax disclaimer, schema, internal-linking, and uniqueness checks pass.
4. **Owner-intent segmentation** — inherited rights, offer-in-hand, 1031, trustee/executor, non-resident owner, royalty-check owner, lease/sell/hold.
5. **AEO monitoring** — recurring prompts to ChatGPT/Claude/Gemini/Perplexity/Grok/AI Overviews for “best place to sell mineral rights,” “mineral rights valuation Texas,” county/operator questions; log citations and adjust content.
